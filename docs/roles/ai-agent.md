# AI Agent

An AI Agent is a trader whose decisions come from a configured hosted large-language-model provider instead of the rule-based engine. An operator converts an Individual into an AI Agent from the trader detail page, choosing a provider and one of that provider's models. The trader keeps trading as a rule-based Individual until it is converted.

## Provider decisions

- A hosted coordinator runs provider inference beside the market loop. Each eligible AI Agent has at most one request in flight, and requests run outside the market transaction so they never delay a two-second cycle.
- Each agent decides a configurable number of times per trading day rather than on every cycle. The decisions are spread across the day, and the last one is an end-of-day planning call: its optional Big Investment and orders are stored and applied at the opening cycle of the next trading day, revalidated against the market state at that time. A plan whose target day never opens for the agent is discarded rather than applied late.
- For each turn the coordinator builds a fresh market snapshot, loads the cached project rules, sends one request with no conversation history, and strictly parses the reply. The snapshot includes current exposure, residual executable asks, active and allowed price bounds, the automated-buy envelope, price-time priority ceilings, cancellable open orders, eligible Big Investment opportunities, and feedback from the previous application. Each opportunity states the current price and exact minimum and maximum funding bounds. When a higher residual ask cannot be crossed without jumping earlier demand, the envelope can instead guide a passive bid at the safe priority ceiling.
- Every company snapshot also includes the latest financial report and its deltas, profitability, stability, volatility, closure risk, management outlook, the newest effective audit and its complete component evidence, and normalized momentum, order-flow, industry, audit, fundamental, and final directional scores. The agent may reach its own conclusion from that evidence; the supplied score is context, not an automatic order.
- The reply must be exactly one JSON object with `summary`, `cancelOrderIds`, `bigInvestment`, `orders`, and `predictions`. `bigInvestment` is either `null` or one company id, an exact whole-share quantity inside the advertised bounds, and a reason. Each new order carries a side, company id, positive integer quantity, a signed price-offset percentage from the company's current market price, and reason. Each prediction names a company, Up or Down direction, confidence, the required fixed horizon, an optional target price, and a reason. Prose-only replies, Markdown fences, missing or unknown fields, a non-positive quantity or company id, a price offset at or below -100 percent, duplicate cancellation ids, and unknown sides make the whole reply invalid. A `null` investment, empty orders array, and empty predictions array remain a valid explicit decision to wait without claiming an unsupported forecast.
- The model authors side, company, price offset, quantity, and reason. The backend resolves the offset into a limit price against the current market price when the decision is applied and clamps that price onto the allowed band, so a slow decision cannot commit to a stale price; it does not otherwise resize the order, and it records why an order was rejected.
- After reacquiring the market lock, the coordinator processes requested cancellations, then the optional Big Investment, then new orders. The cancellation list must be capped and unique; only the agent's own open or partially filled cancellable orders are eligible, and forced margin or loan orders are never cancelled. A successful investment immediately spends settled cash, mints settled shares, and may change the company price, so the backend rebuilds exposure, prices, reservations, cash, and margin before validating orders. A rejected investment does not block otherwise valid orders.
- Successfully parsed orders are applied independently, so one rejected order never blocks another valid one. Buys must fit the shared automated policy as well as current executable supply, price-time priority, LULD, exposure, size, cash, reservation, and margin limits; sells remain subject to ownership and ordinary market checks. Short selling is unavailable, so a model cannot sell beyond the agent's uncommitted holdings. Resolved prices and accepted quantities persist to the order book and are never re-priced by ordinary order maintenance. They remain fixed until fill, agent cancellation, automated expiry, or cancellation by a structural rule such as an invalid price range or stock split. See [Participant rules](../participant-rules.md) and [LULD price controls](../rules/luld.md).
- The trader's retained risk profile shapes the supplied fundamental score and its buy envelope. Low risk emphasizes company quality and uses cash only, Medium risk balances quality and growth and also uses cash only, while High risk emphasizes confident growth and may use limited margin. The hosted model still chooses whether to follow that evidence and must provide every explicit order.
- Cancellation, Big Investment, and order outcomes are stored with the call and included in the next stateless snapshot. An order rejection includes a stable code plus any applicable minimum quantity, maximum quantity, price bounds, or budget ceiling, so the model can correct the next decision without relying on hidden conversation history.
- A malformed reply is retried a bounded number of times within the same decision before it counts as a failure, because resending the same request often returns valid JSON. A provider or parsing failure then leaves the AI Agent idle in a visible Error state; there is no rule-based fallback. A rejected key surfaces the same Error state and retries on a longer authentication window, while a provider with no key configured in Settings stays idle until one is added. Transient errors back off before retrying. Timeout, response-token cap, malformed-response retries, and transport retries use per-provider overrides when present and otherwise inherit the global AI settings.

## Configuration and observability

- The provider, model, and maximum decisions per trading day are set per trader on the detail page; the cadence defaults to three. The provider connection key is not a per-trader value: it is entered once per provider in Settings, stored without encryption, never returned by the API, and never written to a log.
- Conversion is reversible. Converting back to Individual cancels any in-flight request, cancels the trader's open orders, deletes the configuration, and resumes rule-based decisions.
- Every attempted request is recorded before the call and updated with the raw response, parsed decision, explicit predictions, application outcome, timing, and token usage. Retries for the same scheduled decision share an attempt-group id, use consecutive attempt numbers, and record a normalized failure category; a later scheduled cycle starts a new group. The detail page lists this history newest first with server paging and loads a call's full request and response only when it is opened. It also summarizes decision quality for the trader: capital deployed through executed buys, the share of proposed orders accepted, and the share of provider replies parsed successfully.
- Provider/model forecast quality is reported only for mature, split-safe predictions in a common comparison window. Directional accuracy and Brier score include call- or trading-day-clustered uncertainty; a comparison with fewer than five clusters is explicitly marked insufficient instead of showing a misleading interval.
- Call history is retained through provider edits, conversion, pause, restart, and market departure. A full market reset removes it along with the configuration, and active-market reads never mix it with another run.

## Lifecycle

- An AI Agent shares the enforced non-decision lifecycle of an Individual: dividends, the risk-specific automated margin rule, bankruptcy, free-share emissions, starvation-liquidation, and market exit all behave the same. See [Individual](individual.md).
- AI Agents are not selected by automatic fund entry, fund opening, or fund switching. Existing AI fund memberships are retained and can still resolve through ordinary leave or an operator's forced-leave action.
- The random Big Investment event never selects an AI Agent. Direct company funding is available only when the current AI snapshot advertises an eligible opportunity and the model explicitly chooses it.
- New traders that appear through market churn are always rule-based Individuals, so an AI Agent appears only through explicit operator configuration.
